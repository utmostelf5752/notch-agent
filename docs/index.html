Doesn't take over your screen

Hover the notch, type, send. Your editor or browser stays where it was. Esc to close, or pin it if you want it to hang around.

Your agents, not ours

It talks to claude, codex, and Cursor's agent on your machine. Your login, your usage, whatever folder you pointed it at.

ChatGPT without setting up an API

There's a web view logged into chatgpt.com. Sign in once and keep chatting. Same as the site, just from the notch.

You can watch it work

Text shows up as it's written. When it reads a file or runs a command, you see that too. Finished steps collapse into one line you can expand later.

Drag stuff in

Drop a file on the panel or the notch. Or attach a screenshot: the whole screen, or just the window in front of you.

Old chats don't vanish

Conversations get saved with their provider and session. Open one from history and pick up where you left off.

Install

01

Download the DMG and drag Eave to Applications.

02

First launch: Eave isn't signed with a paid Apple developer certificate yet, so macOS blocks it the first time. This is expected. You open it once through Settings, and every launch after that is a normal double-click.

  1. Open Eave from Applications. macOS says it can't verify the developer. Click Done, not Move to Trash.
  2. Go to Apple menu  → System Settings → Privacy & Security and scroll down to Security.
  3. Next to “Eave was blocked”, click Open Anyway. This button only shows for about an hour after step 1, so if it's missing, try opening Eave again.
  4. Enter your login password, click OK, then confirm Open Anyway.

Prefer the terminal? This does the same thing in one line:

xattr -dr com.apple.quarantine /Applications/Eave.app
03

Hover the notch (or press ⌥ space), pick a provider and a working folder, and say hi.
